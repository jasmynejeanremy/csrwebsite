Conversation Starters

There are many risks involved with using technology, so it is important to develop strategies to help your child navigate through online and digital spaces. Some of the topics regarding online safety can be difficult to talk about. However, it is important that open and honest communication occurs to help promote safety and good habits. Here are some conversation starter ideas for discussing online safety with your child:

  • Social media is very pervasive in today’s world, so having a conversation about it in the car or at the dinner table is very natural. Bringing up current topics or encouraging your child to carry values such as confidence and self-worth are great ways to start talking about social media

  • Asking the simple question of “should you treat people differently online compared to the real world?” will help you gauge your child’s views on online behavior

  • Get to know what sites your child likes or things they are curious about. Navigate through sites online to help explore your child’s interests and show them how to navigate safely

  • Show your child resources for them to go to in case they need help and talk about different online scenarios they may encounter

  • Asking What If? Questions are great ways to have engaging conversations with your child that test their critical thinking skills

  • Ask your child what they are worried about and address those issues. Also, be positive and open when talking to your child

  • Talking to your child about video games is also important. Ask about why they like certain games. Discuss topics such as screen time limits, avoiding in-game purchases, and age-appropriate ratings

  • General

    Parents should be clear with their boundaries, but at the same time these discussions should help build trust with your children. Make sure your child knows that you are there to help guide them and serve as a role model of good behavior. Finally, remember to be flexible and to not make any assumptions about you or your child’s knowledge.
