Information about Videogames

Video games are a fun way for children to develop coordination and planning skills. However, it is important to set limits and make sure your child is safe. From cyberbullying to hidden costs, there are various risks involved with video games that you should be aware of. Keep the following in mind when monitoring your childâ€™s gameplay:

  • Carefully research game ratings and check if a specific game has elements that you are not comfortable with

  • Set time limits and encourage your child to take breaks from video games

  • Cyber-bullying on a gaming platform can be common, so be vigilant of any games with chat or communication capabilities; encourage positive and healthy communication

  • Buy a webcam cover and ensure that the webcams are off when your child is online

  • Make sure your child does not have access to your credit card information so that you can avoid any in-game surprise purchases, and/or make sure purchases require a password that only you know

  • Gaming with your child can help encourage safe play and open communication
