Fun Offline Activities!

In case you’re dealing with as much screen fatigue as everyone else right now, here’s a list of offline activities accessible to kids!

  • Knitting/Crocheting

    If you have some yarn around the house, knitting and crocheting are good ways to relax and make something, and it’s a great way to make presents. Here’s a video of how to knit for beginners. To see if you like it, start out with some regular yarn and you can use pens or pencils as improvised knitting needles, as long as they have a similar shape to knitting needles. Crochet is like knitting, but with one hook instead of two needles, which lets you do more things freestyle once you have some experience. You will need some yarn, and you can start by using your fingers! You can also crochet using a crochet hook; here’s where to learn how.

    There are lots of patterns for knitting and crocheting projects available online or on YouTube, so search around and see what looks fun!

  • Outdoor games

    Playing games outside with friends or siblings can be a great way to have fun and become closer friends. Some classics are hopscotch, jump rope, Simon Says, and red light, green light. Here are even more!

  • Drawing guides

    Drawing is something you can do either on your own or with friends, and it is perfect for rainy days. You can draw whatever you want, but here are some links if you want some inspiration to get you started:

    • https://design.tutsplus.com/articles/100-easy-drawing-tutorials--cms-28365
    • https://www.easydrawingtips.com/
    • Nature Hikes

    Spending some time walking in nature can help you relax. You could use it as a chance to disconnect from screens, or if you’d rather you could use technology to learn more about the world around you, like identifying animal tracks, bird calls, and types of plants. Here are some tips for beginner hikers, if you want more advice before you get started!

  • Juggling

    Learn how to juggle with this video! You can make your own juggling balls out of socks and rice. Also, if you’re having trouble catching the balls, you can give yourself more time by throwing them up higher, or you can try with tissues, which almost float in the air.
